# The GoMad Public Roadmap

GoMad is a hard fork of an upstream agile-AI-driven-development framework. The
v1.1 milestone is focused on the rename, the slim down, the credit, and the
branding. New agents and skills beyond what shipped upstream are intentionally
deferred to a later milestone.

<div class="roadmap-container">

  <h2 class="roadmap-section-title">Milestone 1 (v1.1.0) - In Progress</h2>

  <div class="roadmap-future">
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>Atomic Rename</h4>
      <p>Complete file-system and text-level rename to the GoMad namespace so nothing upstream leaks into the published package.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>Credit and Legal</h4>
      <p>Preserve the upstream MIT license byte-identically, rewrite the trademark file under nominative fair use, and credit the upstream authors prominently in the README.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>Slim Down</h4>
      <p>Remove the external-modules manifest and any builder or web-bundle residue that GoMad does not ship or support.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>Branding</h4>
      <p>New banner, wordmark, favicon, and CLI startup banner so the surface visually matches the rename.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <h2 class="roadmap-section-title">Milestone 2 and beyond</h2>

  <div class="roadmap-future">
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>GoMad-specific Agents</h4>
      <p>New agents that live only in GoMad and extend the core agile workflow for our use cases.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="roadmap-future-card">
      <h4>GoMad-specific Skills</h4>
      <p>Skills built specifically for GoMad users rather than ported wholesale from upstream.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
